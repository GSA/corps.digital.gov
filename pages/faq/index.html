Frequently Asked Questions

Digital Corps Fellows will be able to work remotely, in-person, or in a hybrid model subject to the needs of their placement agency. Fellows will have the option of working from Washington D.C. where many agencies are headquartered. Your location will be negotiated and determined during the selection process.

Fellows who are not located in Washington, D.C. should expect to travel occasionally for agency work and/or program events. All official travel costs will be covered by the program.

Yes, Fellows’ salary will vary depending on work location (including telework) according to the Office of Personnel Management’s locality pay tables. More information will be provided to candidates before and during the application process.

All Digital Corps Fellows will begin the two-year program at the GS‑9 pay level and receive a recruitment incentive. As an example, with the recruitment incentive, Washington, D.C.-based Fellows will have a starting total salary just above $80,000.

We are not able to accept or consider letters of recommendation or other materials not explicitly requested during the application process.

We recommend that you select the track that most closely matches your skill set. Doing so will help our team thoughtfully pair candidates with agencies and projects. Your track selection will not limit the projects you are considered for and Digital Corps Fellows will have ample opportunity to learn and grow in areas beyond their selected track.

During the selection process, candidates will have the opportunity to indicate their interests and preferences. Ultimately, Fellows’ placements will be based on mutual interest with agencies and alignment of skill sets.

We plan to hire 30 Fellows total across the five tracks for our June 2022 cohort. The exact breakdown among tracks will depend on agency project specifics. We anticipate that the Digital Corps will scale significantly in subsequent cohorts and years.

Digital Corps Fellows will work at the same agency for the duration of the fellowship, though we anticipate you will have opportunities to contribute to multiple high-impact projects over the course of the two years.

Fellows who successfully complete the two-year fellowship will be eligible to convert to full-time, career positions in the competitive service at their agency.

Definitions vary, but internships tend to be shorter in nature than fellowships. The U.S. Digital Corps is a paid, full-time opportunity for two years with a path to a career position in the federal government. Fellows will benefit from a dedicated learning and development curriculum, mentorship, and other support structures to grow both personally and professionally during the fellowship.
