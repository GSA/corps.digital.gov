The Opportunity

Begin your career at the intersection of technology and public service.

The U.S. Digital Corps is a new two‑year fellowship to launch your career at the intersection of technology and public service.

The fellowship is a paid, full‑time position that offers a competitive salary and benefits. Fellows will have the opportunity to convert to a permanent, full-time position upon successful completion of the program. The Digital Corps will support in‑person (Washington, D.C.), hybrid, and distributed Fellows, subject to the telework policies of host agencies.

Digital Corps Fellows will begin the two-year program at the GS‑9 pay level and be eligible for certain incentives. Like all federal employees, salary will be based on Fellows’ location according to OPM’s locality pay tables. Washington, D.C.-based Fellows who receive recruitment incentives they are eligible for will have a starting total salary of $80,XXX. More details about salary and benefits is available on the Apply page.

Who we’re looking for

The U.S. Digital Corps is an early‑career technology opportunity for those from accredited undergraduate and graduate degree programs, reskilling, and other certificate programs. We bring on Fellows in five broad skill tracks: software engineering, data science and analytics product management, design, and cybersecurity.

We recognize that technology is a means, not an end. As such, we’re looking for candidates who aren’t afraid to roll up their sleeves and contribute in any way, technical or otherwise, to create a government that better serves all Americans. This includes collaborating with diverse sets of stakeholders, communicating technical subjects to general audiences, and working iteratively and centering the end user.

  • DevOps, cloud computing, cloud infrastructure
  • Front-end, back-end, full-stack engineering
  • Web and mobile development
  • Prototype and test new features and applications

  • Artificial intelligence, data science
  • Machine learning and other models, data strategy, management, analytics
  • Clean datasets, analysis, natural language processing
  • Data visualization
  • Modern statistical and computational methods

  • Product development, product management, agile software methodologies
  • Stakeholder coordination
  • Product roadmaps, metrics, etc. product vision
  • Strategy

  • UX/UI design
  • Human-centered design, service design, product design
  • User research and testing
  • Customer experience

  • TBD

Deliver high-impact work

Fellows will be matched with federal agencies based on their skill sets and interests and will work on pressing priorities to better serve the public. Priorities include:

  • Economic recovery
  • Racial equity
  • COVID-19 response
  • Health care
  • Climate

Grow personally and professionally

The Digital Corps will invest in Fellows’ career growth with a dedicated learning and development curriculum and individual performance plans, helping you mature as a technologist and sharpen the problem solving skills that will make you more effective in creating impact in government and other large organizations.

Join a supportive, diverse community

Digital Corps Fellows will join a welcoming community of supportive mentors and passionate peers that includes fellow government technologists, and organizations like Presidential Innovation Fellows, U.S. Digital Service, and 18F.
