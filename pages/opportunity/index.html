The Opportunity

Begin your career at the intersection of technology and public service.

The United States Digital Corps is a new two-year fellowship for early-career technologists

{% include_cached sign-up.html %}

Deliver
high-impact work

Fellows will be matched with federal agencies and will use their technology skills to work on pressing priorities to better serve the public. Priorities include:

  • Economic recovery
  • Racial equity
  • COVID-19 response
  • Health care
  • Climate

Grow personally and professionally

The Digital Corps will invest in Fellows’ career growth with a dedicated learning and development curriculum and individual performance plans, helping you mature as a technologist and sharpen the bureaucracy hacking skills that will make you more effective in creating impact in government and other large organizations.

Join a supportive, diverse community

Digital Corps Fellows will join a welcoming community of supportive mentors and passionate peers within the Digital Corps and from organizations like Presidential Innovation Fellows, U.S. Digital Service, and 18F.

Who we’re looking for

The Digital Corps is an early-career technology opportunity for those from undergraduate degree programs, apprenticeships, bootcamps, reskilling, and other training programs. No work experience is required, though we expect candidates to have the requisite technical skills and training.

While we build a big tent around what it means to be a technologist—including software engineering, product management, design, data science and analysis, and cybersecurity—we recognize that technology is a means, not an end. As such, we’re looking for candidates who aren’t afraid to roll up their sleeves and contribute in any way, technical or otherwise, to create a government that better serves all Americans.

Government technology and civic tech is a team sport, and we expect Digital Corps fellows to be empathetic, collaborative, and mission-oriented.
