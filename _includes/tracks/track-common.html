Duties

Summary

The U.S. Digital Corps is a two-year development program for top technology talent that brings highly-skilled, diverse, and mission-driven early career technologists to federal agencies. During this appointment, the fellow is placed in a participating partner federal agency - improving service delivery through technology modernization and transformation - while receiving additional support and development as part of the U.S. Digital Corps program.

The first year of the U.S. Digital Corps fellowship is a Recent Graduates Program - a dynamic, one-year developmental program that promotes careers in the federal government to recent graduates. Recent Graduates who successfully complete the first year Program requirements may be eligible for non-competitive conversion to a permanent position within the civil service for the remaining year of the U.S. Digital Corps fellowship. Permanent placement to their fellowship placement agency or another eligible position is possible after completing the full U.S. Digital Corps program.

Additional information

To be eligible for conversion to the competitive service after completion of the program, a Pathways Recent Graduate must:

  1. Be a U.S. citizen or national (resident of American Samoa or Swains Island)
  2. Successfully complete an academic course of study as evidenced by a qualifying degree or be within 9 months of graduation.
  3. Meet, at the end of the program, the OPM qualification standard for the position to which the Recent Graduate may be converted.
  4. Maintain an acceptable performance under GSA's approved performance management system.

  6. Meet all the training and development requirements outlined in the GSA Pathways policy.

GSA is not obligated to make an offer of employment, nor is the Pathways employee obligated to accept an offer of employment.

How you will be evaluated

You will be evaluated for this job based on how well you meet the qualifications named for the role in the job announcement.

The evaluation process for this job is different from a typical federal job announcement.

The qualifications for this position will be evaluated by subject matter experts. You are being evaluated under the category rating method which means, if you are determined to be qualified, you will be placed into the ‘Best Qualified’, ‘Well Qualified’, or ‘Qualified’ category by Subject Matter Experts (SME) based on your knowledge and experience to the required competencies of this position. If you misrepresent your knowledge and experience in your application package, you will be excluded from consideration for this job.

Apply only if you meet the qualifications.

Resume review:

Subject matter experts will review the first page of education and two (2) pages of work experience on your resume to determine your technical qualifications for this position based on the required competencies.

Practical Written Assessment:

If you pass the resume review, you will be given a track specific written assessment. Your response to the practical assessment will be used to measure your depth of knowledge in relation to the required competencies for the position. Depending on the volume of applications passing this step in the evaluation process, either the top candidates will move into the next assessment phase (with remaining candidates holding in the process) or all passing candidates will move to the next phase.

Qualifying phone interviews:

At this phase, you will have at least one phone interview to further assess whether your experience meets the required competencies. You should expect your interview to cover both technical and non-technical competencies. You may meet with more than one subject matter expert for a determination to be made.

All interviews will be conducted virtually. Live captioning and other accessibility assistance is available for all candidates. If you require accommodations at any point of the application or hiring process, please contact jointts@gsa.gov.

Ranking and preference:

Veteran Preference will be applied to applicants who move forward after the qualifying phone interviews. The “Best Qualified” applicants will be issued to the Selecting Official by the Human Resources Office. Veteran preference eligibles are listed ahead of non-preference eligibles within each quality category. Based upon your category and score, you may be referred to the U.S. Digital Corps for interviews with host agencies.

Future consideration:

We anticipate hiring 30 fellows in 2022. There are many great roles available in the federal government. When we complete our selections, the remaining list of qualified candidates may be shared with additional federal agencies. You will have an opportunity to opt-in if you would like your name, application material, and assessment results shared to be considered for employment with additional agencies. There is no guarantee of further consideration and you can continue to explore job opportunity announcements.
